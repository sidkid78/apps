AI-Powered Innovation: Transforming Home Modification Assessments

At the heart of HOMEase | AI is its proprietary Augmented Reality (AR) assessment tool, a game-changer for accessible home design. The homeowner journey begins with a simple AR scan of a room using their device's camera.

This input is then analyzed by Google Gemini, which identifies accessibility hazards, assigns an accessibility score (0-100), and recommends specific modifications (e.g., widening doorways, installing grab bars). Simultaneously, Fal.ai generates compelling 'after' visualizations, allowing users to clearly envision the proposed changes and understand their impact. This process yields high-intent, pre-qualified leads with detailed project scopes, which homeowners then submit, specifying urgency and budget.

The platform intelligently matches these leads with vetted, qualified local contractors, facilitating seamless communication through an integrated messaging system. Secure payments are processed via Stripe, and a robust review system ensures continuous quality control. Beyond homeowners, contractors benefit from professional profiles, efficient lead management, and a Dynamic Scope Builder that auto-generates proposals from AR data. Administrators gain comprehensive oversight through a dashboard for managing contractor approvals, user accounts, and platform analytics.

A Robust and Scalable Technical Foundation

Our cutting-edge, cloud-native architecture ensures unparalleled reliability, security, and rapid scalability, underpinning our ambitious growth plans. HOMEase | AI is built on a serverless, event-driven architecture hosted entirely on the Google Cloud Platform (GCP), ensuring unparalleled scalability, security, and performance.

  • Frontend: Next.js 15 application deployed on Google Cloud Run, containerized via Docker.
  • Backend: Serverless Google Cloud Functions managing API requests and logic.
  • Data & Storage: Firebase Firestore (NoSQL real-time database) and Google Cloud Storage.
  • Security: Firebase Authentication with NextAuth.js v5 and strict encryption.

Our DevOps pipeline utilizes GitHub Actions with secure, keyless authentication to GCP via Workload Identity Federation, ensuring continuous integration and deployment. For AR implementation, a native mobile application leveraging device LiDAR (ARKit/ARCore) is prioritized for maximum accuracy, with a WebXR-based version providing an accessible fallback option.

Unlocking Federal Partnerships: The OAHMP Opportunity

A significant strategic opportunity lies in partnering with federal grant programs, notably the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development's (HUD) Older Adults Home Modification Program (OAHMP). OAHMP provides substantial grant funding to non-profits and government agencies for low-cost, high-impact home modifications for low-income seniors (age 62+), demonstrating strong federal commitment with $109 million awarded in January 2025.

This program's service model, which requires in-home assessments by licensed Occupational Therapists (OTs) and work by licensed and insured contractors, aligns perfectly with HOMEase | AI's core competencies. Our platform is ideally positioned to act as a crucial technology and service partner to OAHMP grantees.
